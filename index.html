ABOUT US

Most women have a problem coping up with life changes during and after
pregnancy .The proposed application is aimed at helping Pre and Post natal
mothers to monitor their babies growth throughout the whole trimester and a
few months after.

SERVICES


(1-3)months

signs & symptoms:

  • nausea and vomiting
  • cravings and aversion
  • heightened sense of smell
  • mood swings

fetal development

  • fertilization
  • implantation
  • hormone levels increase
  • the neural tube closes
  • baby's head develops
  • baby's nose forms
  • baby's toes appear
  • baby's elbow bends
  • baby's genitals develop
  • baby's fingernails form

activities to be done:

  • Make sure you're really pregnant
  • Take your prenatal vitamin
  • Investigate health insurance
  • Choose a healthcare provider
  • Make a prenatal appointment
  • Consult your provider about medications you're taking
  • If you smoke or take alcohol, quit
  • Cut down on caffeine
  • Make sure your activities are pregnancy-safe
  • Do your best to eat well
  • Learn the signs of a pregnancy problem

(4-6)months

signs & symptoms:

  • round ligament pains
  • nipple changes
  • stretch marks
  • feeling the baby moving

fetal development

  • Urine forms
  • Baby's sex becoming apparent
  • Baby's scalp pattern develops
  • Baby's eyes move
  • Baby's toenails develop
  • Baby begins to hear
  • Baby develops protective coating
  • Baby can suck his or her thumb
  • Baby's hair becomes visible
  • Fingerprints and footprints form
  • Baby's skin is wrinkled
  • Baby responds to your voice
  • Baby's lungs develop

activities to be done:

  • Find a prenatal exercise class
  • Learn about second-trimester prenatal visits and tests
  • Start shopping for maternity clothes
  • Decide whether to hire a professional labor coach (doula)
  • Start moisturizing your belly
  • Prepare your older children
  • Start sleeping on your side
  • Start doing Kegel exercises
  • Avoid unsafe activities

(7-9)months

signs & symptoms:

  • strong kicks from the baby
  • swollen feet
  • leaking from the breasts
  • frequent urination

fetal development

  • Baby's eyes partially open
  • Baby kicks and stretches
  • Baby's hair grows
  • Baby's rapid weight gain begins
  • Baby practices breathing
  • Baby detects light
  • Baby's fingernails grow
  • Baby's skin is pink and smooth
  • Baby takes up most of the amniotic sac
  • Baby might turn head first
  • Baby's toenails grow
  • Baby's chest is prominent
  • Your due date arrives

activities to be done:

  • Pick out Baby's going-home outfit and wash it
  • Find a pediatrician
  • Pack your hospital bag
  • Learn how to use your baby carrier or wrap and stroller

(The baby's first 3 months)

signs & symptoms:

  • lochia
  • fatigue
  • sore,leaking breasts
  • "baby blues"
  • risk of postpartum depression

activities to be done:

  • holding the baby close
  • gently swaying or rocking the baby
  • making swishing or shushing noises
  • swaddling
  • giving the baby opportunities to suck, either during breastfeeding or by using a pacifier
